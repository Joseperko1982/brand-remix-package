# SAS is dead

"SAS is dead."

Not because AI arrived. Because the hiding place got automated away.

SAS: "Same As Same." More variations of the same unremarkable creative. Faster. Cheaper. More.

That model worked when production was the bottleneck. AI removed it.

Now it's obvious: the bottleneck was never production. It was taste.

AI handles the labor. Your brief was always asking for the *thinking*. But SAS never had to charge for thinking — only production.

Agencies still running on "more, faster, cheaper"? They're not AI-powered. They're mediocrity at scale.

The ones who'll own the next decade? They ship taste. They let AI handle production while they focus on decisions, curation, the point of view that can't be automated.

Your move.
